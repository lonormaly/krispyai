# LinkedIn — Launch Post

LinkedIn is underrated for dev tools — low competition, high reach for an honest founder story.
Build-in-public, plainspoken, ⭐ CTA. **Put the repo link in the FIRST COMMENT, not the body** —
LinkedIn throttles posts with outbound links. Buttr stays light here; the register is
founder-honest more than croissant-playful.

**Format:** short lines, generous whitespace, no hashtag soup (2–3 relevant tags max at the end:
#opensource #buildinpublic #indiehackers).

---

## English post

> I got quoted per-seat for a chat widget on my landing page.
>
> I'm one person.
>
> So instead of paying it, I spent [X weeks] building the open-source version. It's called Krispy.
>
> The problem is one every solo founder knows. You want to actually talk to visitors — but your
> options are a dumb bot that answers nothing, or a $100–400/mo tool to get a real human in the
> loop. Priced per seat. With a login your customer never wanted. And every conversation living on
> someone else's servers.
>
> Krispy is the third option: an AI answers the easy questions in your voice — and the moment a
> real person is needed, it hands off to you on Telegram. You reply from your phone, and it shows
> up live in the visitor's chat. The AI is the first touch, never the last word.
>
> It's open source (MIT). Free to self-host on Cloudflare's free tier — no API key, no per-seat
> tax, your data stays in your own account. There's a hosted version too ($19/mo flat) if you'd
> rather not deploy anything, but self-host is free forever. That's the point, not the bait.
>
> I built it in public, and I'm launching it in public. It's early. It probably breaks in ways I
> haven't found yet. If you try it and something's wrong, tell me — I'll fix it today.
>
> And if you find it useful: read the code, fork it, ship it, drop a ⭐. That genuinely makes my
> day.
>
> Repo + demo in the first comment 👇
>
> #opensource #buildinpublic #indiehackers

## Notes
- Swap `[X weeks]` and the per-seat quote for the true story.
- The opening three short lines (quote → "I'm one person" → "so I built it") are the hook — keep
  them terse; LinkedIn shows the first ~3 lines before "see more."
- Reply to every comment. LinkedIn rewards the post that keeps a conversation going for hours.
- Repost your own post ~a week later with the "here's what happened" numbers — build-in-public
  retros perform as well as the launch itself.
