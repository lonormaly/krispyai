## Name-the-villain lines (honest, never childish)

- "Intercom's great at being Intercom. It's just not built for one person who wants to own their
  stack."
- The villain isn't a competitor — it's per-seat pricing, lock-in, and your conversations on
  someone else's servers.
- Most AI support is sold on deflection — talk to customers *less*. Krispy is the opposite: talk
  to more of them, more easily.
- A login your customer never asked for is not a feature.

- **vs Intercom:** "no shade. i'm just free."

- **launch day:** "big day. i'll work the room so you can watch the star counter."
- **footer / sign-off:** "à bientôt 🥐"

## "Steal it, drop a ⭐" invites (the OSS growth loop — founder register)

- Read the code, fork it, ship it. If it's useful, a ⭐ makes my day.
- Rip the Telegram-handoff pattern into your own project. That's what open source is for.
- Self-hosting it somewhere? Tell me where — I want to see it live.
- Found a bug? Open an issue. Fixed a bug? Open a PR. Both make my week.

## Anti-patterns — do NOT ship these (from brand-voice)

- ❌ "revolutionary," "game-changing," "10x," "unlock," "seamless," "AI-powered" as the lead hook,
  "delight your customers," "book a demo," "reach out," "streamline," "cutting-edge."
- ❌ Staccato fragment chains: "Fast. Simple. Open." Write real sentences.
- ❌ Manufactured tagline closers on every section. End when the point's made.
- ❌ Pretending the bot is human. We're proud it's AI *and* proud it hands off.
- ❌ Trashing Intercom/Crisp/Chatwoot as companies. Name the pricing villain, respect the product.
- ❌ Buttr in the load-bearing sentence, on HN, or on Reddit. Croissant is seasoning, not the meal.
